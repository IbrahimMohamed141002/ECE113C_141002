The popular book written by the famous chinese tactician Sun Tzu 2500 years ago. The book contained a detailed explanation and analysis of the Chinese military, from weapons and strategy to rank and discipline. Sun also stressed the importance of intelligence operatives and espionage to the war effort. Because Sun has long been considered to be one of history's finest military tacticians and analysts, his teachings and strategies formed the basis of advanced military training for centuries to come.

Mesopotamia literally means "between the rivers" in ancient Greek, which refers to what we now call Iraq. Mesopotamia had a long rich history, and one of the oldest civilizations originated there. As old as, if not older than, the Ancient Egyptian civilzation.
The Mongol Empire existed during the 13th and 14th centuries, and it was the fastest growing empire in history, conquering lands from Mongolia and China all the way to Syria and the middle of Europe. The mongols were known for their ruthlessness in battle and excellent archery, and their seemingly endless numbers. All of this contributed to them being able to challenge and conquer the strongest empires of that time.
